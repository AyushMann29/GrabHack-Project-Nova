Details we need from you:

  • Partner Type: Select whether you are a Merchant or a Driver. This helps the system know what type of user you are.
  • Earnings (Value): Enter your average monthly earnings on the platform. Must be 0 or higher. This helps assess financial eligibility.
  • Earnings (Stability Type): Choose how consistent your earnings are: Stable, Variable, or Seasonal. Stable income is usually favorable.
  • Performance Rating (Avg): Enter your average performance rating on the platform from 1 to 5 (decimals allowed, e.g., 4.5). High ratings indicate reliability.
  • Time on Platform (Months): Enter the number of months you have been active on the platform. Longer activity shows experience and commitment.
  • Order/Trip Volume: Enter the total number of orders or trips you have completed. Higher volumes indicate activity and reliability.
  • Financial Activity (Score): Enter a score between 0 and 1 representing your financial activity or responsibility on the platform. Higher scores indicate better behavior.
  • Earnings Volatility: Enter a number showing how much your earnings fluctuate. Lower numbers indicate more stable income.
  • On-Time Loan Repayments: Enter the number of loans you have repaid on time. Timely repayments show financial responsibility.
  • Operational Anomaly Score: Enter a score between 0 and 1 that shows unusual activity in your platform operations. Lower scores are better.

Results we provide you:

  • Based on the information you provide in the form (earnings, ratings, time on platform, etc.), the system predicts whether you are eligible for a loan or not. Evaluation metrics returned for the model used are:
  • Accuracy: The percentage of correct predictions out of all predictions. It shows how often the model predicts correctly.
  • Precision: The percentage of predicted "Eligible" users who are actually eligible. High precision means fewer false positives.
  • Recall (Sensitivity): The percentage of actual eligible users that were correctly predicted as eligible. High recall means fewer false negatives.
  • F1 Score: The harmonic mean of precision and recall. It provides a single metric to summarize the model’s performance, balancing false positives and false negatives.
